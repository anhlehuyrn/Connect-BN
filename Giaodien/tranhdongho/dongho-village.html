üìç Origin & Location

Dong Ho folk painting originates from Dong Ho Village in Bac Ninh Province, situated along the banks of the Duong River in northern Vietnam. For centuries, the village has been known as a cradle of traditional woodblock printing.

ü™µ Printing Technique

Each painting is made using hand-carved woodblocks, with separate blocks for line art and each color layer. Artisans print the outlines first, then apply colors layer by layer, requiring patience, precision, and experience.

üå± Meaning Then & Now

Past

Dong Ho paintings served as affordable, meaningful decorations for the Lunar New Year. They reflected villagers' wishes for a peaceful life, virtuous behavior, strong family ties, and agricultural abundance.

Today

The craft highlights early eco-friendly art practices, with handmade paper and natural pigments long before sustainability became a global concern. It represents Vietnam's traditional wisdom in living harmoniously with nature.
